Constellations

Constellations - groups of stars that occupy certain areas in the sky and form some kind of figure. Constellations are usually formed by the brightest stars visible to the naked eye, although these figures are sometimes difficult to grasp, and some shapeless and dim.

constellations all over the sky

The figures of the constellations are imaginary, they just help to better remember and find them in the sky. Each star actually exists on its own and is in no way connected with others. Two stars of the same constellation, located close to the sky, can actually be removed from us at very different distances - one for ten light years, and the other for a thousand.

Therefore, the division of stars into a constellation is purely conditional, for the convenience of search and mapping. So the water on Earth is conditionally divided into oceans, although they all form one World Ocean. But in ancient times, people actually saw figures of people and animals in star placers, and gave them names. Many of them have survived to our time, and the boundaries of the constellations share these figures.

10 most famous constellations

Big Dipper

Big Dipper is perhaps the most famous constellation located in the northern hemisphere. Each ladle with a handle was in the sky, at least once. The area of the Ursa Major is 1280 square degrees, the third largest. You can see 125 with the naked eye.

Asterism (an easily distinguishable group of stars) The Big Bucket, has many other names. And this is not the only asterism in the constellation Ursa Major. The history of the origin of the constellation is described in ancient Greek myths. Zeus saved the beautiful nymph Callisto from the wrath of the goddess Hera. To do this, he had to turn her into a bear.

Ursa Minor

The constellation Ursa Minor is near-polar and is located in the Northern Hemisphere. It is easy to find next to the constellation Ursa Major. We can say that these constellations are neighbors.

Year-round available for observation. At the moment, there is the North Pole of the world. Asterisms: Small Bucket, Guardians of the Pole. If you go back to myths, then Ursa Minor is a dog of the beautiful nymph Callisto. Zeus turned her into a bear with the mistress. Then he threw them into heaven, where they were able to gain eternal life.

Hercules

The location of Hercules is the northern hemisphere. The area of the constellation is 1225 square degrees. It is considered one of the most recognizable. The trapezoid is the torso of the titanium, the most noticeable part. Residents of Russia can observe it in its entirety, only a certain part of the constellation is hiding behind the horizon at the time of the lower climax, the most favorable time is June.

The original name is Kneeling. The ancient poet Aratus described the constellation as a suffering husband, the causes of suffering were unknown. In V BC century renamed the constellation, it began to be called Heracles. Later it was called Hercules.

Pegasus

Pegasus is a large constellation. Location - Northern Hemisphere. The area is 1120.8 square degrees. Without the use of any devices, 166 stars can be considered. The best time is the end of summer, the beginning of autumn.

Pegasus is a large square with a scattering of stars that look more like tentacles. Therefore, only people with a good imagination can watch the winged horse. In ancient Greek myths, Pegasus is a winged horse. After Perseus beheaded the Medusa of the Gorgon, droplets of her blood turned into a horse.

Cassiopeia

Cassiopeia is located in the Northern Hemisphere, so in the middle latitudes you can observe it all year round, the best time is autumn. The constellation is similar to the letter W, its area is 598 square degrees, the number of visible stars is 90. Its silhouette is formed by 5 brightest stars.

The constellation is named after the wife of King Kefey. Cassiopeia was also the mother of Andromeda. This boastful woman was punished. She was tied to the throne, and she revolved around the pole, once a day Cassiopeia was in an inverted position, upside down.

Hydra

Hydra is the longest constellation, its area is 1300 square meters. degrees. Location - Southern Hemisphere. In Russia, it is best observed at the end of winter or spring. Residents of southern cities will be able to fully see the constellation. 229 stars can be observed without the help of a telescope and binoculars, but they do not differ in special brightness. The constellation has many interesting stars: Alpha Hydra, Gamma, Xi Hydra, as well as open clusters.

Prototype - Water Snake. Apollo's raven went to get water and was absent for too long. As an apology for the delay, the bird brought a snake to God. An angry Apollo threw a raven, a snake and a bowl of water into the sky. So the constellations Raven and Hydra appeared. According to another version, Hydra is the enemy of Hercules, the seven-headed monster.

Virgo

The constellation Virgo is the second largest, its area is 1294 square degrees. The location is the equator between the constellations of Leo and Libra. Virgo is also known for the fact that the point of the autumnal equinox is located here. In the staninas atlases, the constellation is depicted in the form of a girl who holds a spike of wheat. Of course, it is unlikely that an ordinary person will be able to see a similar picture in the sky. There is a guideline by which it is easy to find this constellation - this is a star of the first magnitude of Spica. A total of 171 stars can be seen with the naked eye.

Centaur

The centaur is a rather impressive constellation in area (1060 square degrees). Residents of the Northern Hemisphere will not be able to enjoy its beauty.

Its location is the Southern Hemisphere, a line from Ursa Major to the constellation Virgo. In Russia, the following principle applies: the farther south the city, the better the constellation is visible. But in our country there is no way to see it in its entirety. The shape of a constellation resembles a centaur, it is a lot of bright stars. According to Greek myths, the wise centaur Chiron is the son of the supreme deity Kronos and the beautiful nymph Filira. There is another type - the centaur Foul, Heracles sent him to heaven. He shot a centaur with a poisoned arrow.

Cepheus

The location of Cepheus is the Northern Hemisphere. Its area is 588 square degrees, with the naked eye you can see 148 stars. Its closest neighbor is Ursa Minor, which everyone will certainly find, even not knowing anything about astronomy.

The shape of Cepheus is an irregular pentagon. There are no bright stars, however, in Russia it can be observed all year round. Cepheus is famous for the fact that in the future the world North Pole will move here. True, this will happen in 1000 years. In Greek mythology, there is a version of the appearance of the constellation. His prototype is the Ethiopian king Cepheus. Scientists do not agree with this fact, since there is confirmation that the constellation appeared much later.

The Dragon

The dragon is one of the largest, its area is 1083 square degrees. Distinguishing it is quite difficult. Location - Northern Hemisphere, the area between the Small and Big Dipper. Dragon stars are dim, faint, the number of stars exceeding 6m (magnitude of brightness measurement), 80.

If you look at the sky in the Ursa Major area, you can see a long curved line that ends with a quadrangle. This is the head of a dragon. It is best to observe this constellation in summer and autumn, from May to December. The history of the origin of the constellation is shrouded in myths and secrets. According to one version, a huge beast decided to fight with the Olympic gods. Athena was very angry with him and threw the snake into the sky. And so the constellation of the Dragon appeared.
